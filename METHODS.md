# Research Methods
© Applied Consciousness Lab (ACL)

This document defines the standard methodology used when conducting experiments within the **Testing-Theories** repository.

The goal is to ensure that experiments are:

- consistent
- transparent
- reproducible
- scientifically grounded

All research conducted within this repository should follow the methodological standards described below.

---

# Experiment Design

Each experiment should include the following components:

• a clearly defined hypothesis  
• relevant datasets  
• defined variables  
• statistical or analytical methods  
• documented results  

Experiments should follow a standardized structure to allow comparison across studies and to support long-term research continuity.

Each experiment should also be recorded in **EXPERIMENT_REGISTRY.md**.

---

# Hypothesis Development

Hypotheses tested in this repository should originate from the research frameworks developed by the Applied Consciousness Lab, including:

• Emotional Physics (EP)  
• Global Safety Standard (GSS)  
• Societal Thermodynamics Framework (STF)  
• Societal Entropy Index (SEI)

Hypotheses must be stated clearly and in a **testable format**.

Example structure:

```
If condition X increases,
then outcome Y should change
according to mechanism Z.
```

Where possible, hypotheses should also include predicted **directionality** (positive correlation, negative correlation, threshold effect, etc.).

---

# Data Collection

Whenever possible, experiments should rely on **publicly available datasets** to ensure transparency and reproducibility.

Examples include:

• World Bank data  
• World Values Survey  
• Transparency International  
• United Nations datasets  
• Freedom House metrics  
• OECD datasets  
• Our World in Data  

Datasets used in experiments must be documented in **DATA_SOURCES.md**.

When datasets require preprocessing, the transformation steps should also be documented.

---

# Analytical Methods

Depending on the research question, analysis may include:

• correlation analysis  
• regression modeling  
• time-series analysis  
• cross-national comparison  
• historical trend analysis  
• clustering analysis  
• composite index construction  

Researchers should clearly describe the analytical method used in each experiment.

Where statistical techniques are applied, assumptions and limitations should be noted.

# Interdisciplinary Evidence Layers

Testing-Theories integrates insights from several scientific disciplines in order to evaluate theoretical frameworks.

These domains provide empirical grounding for system-level hypotheses.

## Epigenetics

Research on how environmental conditions influence gene expression and long-term biological regulation.

Relevant research areas include:

• stress biology  
• gene–environment interaction  
• trauma transmission across generations  
• biological responses to environmental instability  

## Neuroscience

Research exploring how the human brain processes safety, threat, and regulation.

Key topics include:

• stress response mechanisms  
• emotional regulation  
• cognitive bias and perception  
• neurological responses to social instability  

## Complexity Science

Research focused on nonlinear systems and collapse dynamics in complex adaptive systems.

Relevant areas include:

• feedback loops  
• tipping points  
• network dynamics  
• resilience and systemic failure patterns  

These disciplines provide supporting evidence when evaluating the behavioral and societal dynamics proposed in ACL frameworks.

---

# Documentation of Results

Each experiment should produce documented results including:

• the hypothesis tested  
• datasets used  
• methods applied  
• key findings  
• limitations and uncertainties  

Results should be summarized in **EXPERIMENT_REGISTRY.md** and may reference additional documentation within the repository.

Negative or inconclusive results should also be recorded to prevent confirmation bias.

---

# Reproducibility

Experiments should be designed so that independent researchers can reproduce the analysis using the same data and methodology.

Where applicable, researchers should document:

• dataset sources  
• preprocessing steps  
• analytical techniques  
• statistical assumptions  

Transparency is prioritized over complexity.

---

# Research Philosophy

Testing-Theories operates under a simple principle:

**Theory must face evidence.**

Frameworks developed within the Applied Consciousness Lab are evaluated through empirical research, interdisciplinary science, and open documentation.

The purpose of this repository is not to prove theories correct, but to **test them rigorously and refine them based on evidence**.
