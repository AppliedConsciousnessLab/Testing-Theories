# Feedback Loops
© Applied Consciousness Lab (ACL)

Feedback loops occur when the output of a system influences its own future behavior.

They are fundamental mechanisms governing system stability or instability.

---

# Positive Feedback

Positive feedback amplifies change.

Examples:

• bank runs during financial crises  
• viral spread of misinformation  
• rapid social polarization  

Positive feedback can accelerate instability once certain thresholds are crossed.

---

# Negative Feedback

Negative feedback stabilizes systems.

Examples:

• regulatory institutions  
• checks and balances in governance  
• ecological predator–prey balance  

Negative feedback mechanisms help maintain equilibrium.

---

# Importance for Societal Systems

Stable societies tend to maintain strong negative feedback mechanisms.

When these mechanisms weaken, positive feedback loops can accelerate instability.

Understanding feedback dynamics is critical when evaluating systemic resilience.
