# Resilience Models
© Applied Consciousness Lab (ACL)

Resilience refers to the ability of a system to absorb disturbance
while maintaining core functionality.

Resilient systems can adapt and reorganize without collapsing.

---

# Characteristics of Resilient Systems

Resilient systems typically demonstrate:

• distributed decision-making  
• adaptive feedback mechanisms  
• institutional legitimacy  
• strong information integrity  
• social cohesion  

---

# Recovery Dynamics

When systems experience instability, recovery often depends on:

• restoring trust in institutions  
• rebuilding social cohesion  
• stabilizing economic conditions  
• strengthening governance structures  

These factors align with principles explored within the
Global Safety Standard framework.
