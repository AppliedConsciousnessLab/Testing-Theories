# Collapse Patterns in Complex Systems
© Applied Consciousness Lab (ACL)

Many complex systems exhibit similar collapse patterns despite operating in
different domains.

Examples include:

• ecological systems
• financial markets
• political institutions
• civilizations

These systems often experience prolonged stability followed by rapid transition.

---

# Common Collapse Indicators

Research has identified several indicators that often precede systemic collapse:

• declining institutional legitimacy  
• rising inequality  
• reduced trust in governance  
• information instability  
• weakening regulatory mechanisms  

These indicators are relevant to frameworks such as the Societal Entropy Index.

---

# Phase Transitions

Complex systems may undergo phase transitions where the system reorganizes
into a different state.

Examples include:

• democratic breakdown  
• economic restructuring  
• ecosystem collapse  

Understanding these transitions is central to predicting systemic risk.
