# Key Analytical Sources

## Authoritarian Stability and Structural Fragility

Kotkin, Stephen.  
**“The Weakness of the Strongmen: What Really Threatens Authoritarians?”**  
Foreign Affairs, January/February 2026.  
Published December 16, 2025.

Summary:

The article analyzes the structural vulnerabilities of authoritarian regimes despite their apparent strength. Kotkin argues that authoritarian systems often appear stable but are highly dependent on elite cohesion, information control, and economic performance.

Key insights relevant to the Societal Entropy Index (SEI):

• authoritarian regimes suppress signals of instability until crises emerge suddenly  
• elite fragmentation is a major driver of regime collapse  
• economic stagnation and corruption weaken institutional legitimacy  
• information distortion reduces adaptive capacity within governing systems

These insights align with SEI hypotheses regarding institutional legitimacy, information integrity, and systemic stability.

Source:

https://www.foreignaffairs.com
