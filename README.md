# Testing Theories

This repository serves as the empirical testing environment for several research frameworks developed within the Applied Consciousness Lab.

The goal of this repository is to evaluate theoretical models using real-world data, cross-national comparisons, historical case studies, and statistical analysis.

The repository functions as a structured research laboratory where hypotheses are tested, results are documented, and frameworks are refined through evidence.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this repository is to move theoretical frameworks into empirical testing.

Experiments conducted here are designed to:

- evaluate correlations between societal indicators
- test predictions derived from theoretical models
- analyze cross-national and historical datasets
- refine frameworks through evidence-based research

The long-term objective is to determine whether measurable patterns exist that can improve understanding of societal stability and systemic risk.

---

## Research Frameworks

### Global Safety Standard (GSS)

Research exploring the relationship between safety conditions and societal stability.

---

### Emotional Physics (EP)

A framework examining how emotional regulation, perception, and behavioral dynamics influence individual and collective outcomes.

---

### Societal Entropy Index (SEI)

A systems model that evaluates civilizational stability through multiple measurable domains:

- biological foundations
- economic structure
- institutional legitimacy
- social cohesion
- demographic stability
- information integrity

---

### Holistic Testing

Experiments that analyze interactions across multiple frameworks simultaneously.
