# Research Principles
© Applied Consciousness Lab (ACL)

The **Testing-Theories** repository exists to empirically evaluate theoretical frameworks related to societal stability, systemic dynamics, and civilizational resilience.

Research conducted within this repository follows several guiding principles designed to ensure scientific integrity, transparency, and reproducibility.

---

## Evidence First

Hypotheses should be evaluated using **real-world data whenever possible**.  
Claims must be supported by measurable indicators, documented datasets, or clearly described analytical methods.

---

## Transparency

All experiments should clearly document:

• hypotheses  
• methods  
• datasets used  
• analytical procedures  
• results  

The goal is to allow others to understand exactly how conclusions were reached.

---

## Reproducibility

Research should be structured so that results can be reproduced by independent researchers.

Where possible, experiments should include:

• dataset references  
• analysis scripts  
• step-by-step methodology  
• versioned documentation

Scientific value increases when results can be independently verified.

---

## Open Inquiry

Unexpected results, contradictory findings, and failed hypotheses are valuable scientific outcomes.

This repository exists to **test theories, not protect them**.

If a hypothesis fails under empirical evaluation, that result should be documented honestly and used to refine the model.

---

## Interdisciplinary Approach

The systems explored within this repository are complex and require insights from multiple disciplines.

Research may integrate perspectives from:

• systems theory  
• sociology  
• economics  
• psychology  
• complexity science  
• political science  
• network science

Understanding societal stability requires examining how multiple domains interact.

---

## Relationship to ACL Research Frameworks

The Testing-Theories repository functions as the **empirical laboratory** for theoretical frameworks developed within the Applied Consciousness Lab research ecosystem.

Primary frameworks currently evaluated include:

**Emotional Physics (EP)**  
Explores how emotional regulation and threat perception influence individual and collective behavior.

**Global Safety Standard (GSS)**  
Defines structural safety conditions required for institutions and systems to remain stable and humane.

**Societal Thermodynamics (STF)**  
Models how safety distribution influences the stability and entropy of civilizations.

**Societal Entropy Index (SEI)**  
A proposed measurement framework designed to quantify systemic instability using observable indicators.

Testing-Theories provides the environment where hypotheses derived from these frameworks can be evaluated against real-world data.
