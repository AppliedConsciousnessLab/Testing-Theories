# Emotional Physics (EP)

Emotional Physics proposes that emotional regulation, perception, and behavioral feedback loops influence system stability.

Evidence domains used to evaluate EP include:

## Neuroscience

Research areas:

• threat perception  
• emotional regulation  
• cognitive decision-making  
• stress response mechanisms  

Relevant biological structures include:

• amygdala  
• prefrontal cortex  
• hippocampus  

These mechanisms influence how individuals interpret and respond to environmental conditions.

## Epigenetics

Epigenetics studies how environmental conditions influence gene expression.

Relevant research areas include:

• stress biology  
• trauma transmission  
• gene–environment interaction  
• biological responses to long-term instability  

These mechanisms may explain how prolonged environmental conditions influence human behavioral patterns.

# Global Safety Standard (GSS)

The Global Safety Standard proposes that safety conditions are foundational for stable human systems.

Evidence domains used to evaluate GSS include:

## Systems Science

Systems science examines how complex systems stabilize, destabilize, and recover.

Relevant concepts include:

• feedback loops  
• tipping points  
• network dynamics  
• systemic resilience  

These concepts help explain how institutional and social systems respond to stress and instability.

## Governance Research

Research areas include:

• institutional legitimacy  
• rule of law  
• corruption dynamics  
• public trust in governance  

These factors influence long-term societal stability.

# Societal Entropy Index (SEI)

The Societal Entropy Index is a systems model designed to evaluate civilizational stability using measurable indicators.

Evidence domains used to evaluate SEI include:

## Systems Science

Complex systems theory provides models for:

• systemic collapse  
• instability thresholds  
• cascading failures  
• resilience and recovery dynamics  

## Cross-National Datasets

Empirical testing may use data from sources such as:

• World Bank  
• United Nations  
• OECD  
• Transparency International  
• World Values Survey  
• Freedom House  

These datasets allow statistical testing of correlations between societal indicators and stability outcomes.

# Research Philosophy

The Applied Consciousness Lab separates **theoretical frameworks** from **empirical evidence**.

Frameworks are developed within ACL research repositories.

Evidence supporting or challenging those frameworks is collected and analyzed within the **Testing-Theories** repository.

This separation ensures that:

• theories remain testable  
• evidence remains transparent  
• research remains open to refinement
