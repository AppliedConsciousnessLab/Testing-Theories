# Geopolitical Entropy Test Framework
Societal Entropy Index (SEI) Experimental Design

This document outlines potential empirical tests for evaluating geopolitical entropy indicators.

These tests are intended for use in the Testing-Theories repository under the SEI research folder.

---

## Test 1: Chokepoint Disruption Impact

### Question

Do global economic shocks correlate strongly with disruptions at key logistical chokepoints?

### Data Sources

- global oil shipping data
- maritime disruption records
- commodity price volatility
- energy futures markets

### Method

1. Identify chokepoint disruption events
2. Measure price volatility before and after event
3. Compare magnitude of economic impact

### Expected Outcome

Systems heavily dependent on chokepoints exhibit stronger economic shock responses.

---

## Test 2: Cost Asymmetry Sustainability

### Question

Does large cost asymmetry between attack and defense correlate with long-term strategic advantage?

### Data Sources

- military procurement data
- defense spending reports
- conflict outcome datasets

### Method

1. Calculate attack/defense cost ratios
2. Compare with conflict duration and outcomes
3. Evaluate sustainability of defensive expenditures

### Expected Outcome

High defensive cost asymmetry correlates with strategic fatigue and system instability.

---

## Test 3: Institutional Trust and Instability

### Question

Do declines in institutional trust predict political instability events?

### Data Sources

- World Values Survey
- Edelman Trust Barometer
- political instability datasets
- protest frequency databases

### Method

1. Track trust trends over time
2. compare with civil unrest indicators
3. identify threshold values

### Expected Outcome

Low trust thresholds correlate with increased instability.

---

## Test 4: Information Disorder and Polarization

### Question

Does increased misinformation correlate with political polarization and instability?

### Data Sources

- social media datasets
- misinformation tracking databases
- political polarization indexes

### Method

1. measure misinformation prevalence
2. compare with polarization scores
3. track downstream political effects

### Expected Outcome

Higher information entropy predicts greater social fragmentation.

---

## Test 5: Cooling System Strength

### Question

Do societies with strong institutional cooling systems demonstrate greater long-term stability?

### Data Sources

- democracy indexes
- press freedom indexes
- inequality metrics
- diplomatic engagement records

### Method

1. construct cooling system score
2. compare with stability metrics
3. evaluate long-term system resilience

### Expected Outcome

Societies with strong cooling systems maintain lower entropy levels and greater resilience.
